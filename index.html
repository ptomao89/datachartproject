Complaints of illegal housing in Brooklyn heavily concentrated in just 5 community districts

By: Pete Tomao, Nov. 12, 2021

Unlicensed basement units have proliferated in Brooklyn and Queens. City officials estimate there are roughly 50,000 unlicensed basement units throughout New York.

311 recorded 13,213 complaints of illegal housing conversions in Brooklyn (Dataset linked here) . It is the third most reported complaint in Brooklyn. Five Brooklyn community districts account for half of all illegal conversion complaints.

Of those 5 districts, 3 have the highest estimated number of unlicensed units in the entire city(East New York, East Flatbush and Canarsie).

“Enforcement is a tactic the city can take [but] unlikely of course to resolve the issue...the only viable option for the city at this point is to launch an aggressive basement legalization program” --Ryan Chavez, basement conversion project manager, Cypress Hills Local Development Corporation
